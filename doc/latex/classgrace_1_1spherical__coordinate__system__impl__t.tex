\doxysubsection{Detailed Description}
Implementation of coordinate system class for spherical grids. 

Spherical coordinates in grace are implemented using a central patch of Cartesian coordinates which corresponds with tree 0 in the p4est connectivity. The cartesian patch is surrounded by P4\+EST\+\_\+\+FACES (4 in 2D, 6 in 3D) wedges (each of which is a tree) where the coordinates go from Cartesian to Spherical along the logical radius. The logical coordinates in each tree are as usual indicated by $(\zeta,\eta,\xi)$ and each span $[0,1]$. There is then a second layer of wedges where the coordinates are purely spherical in each patch, with the option of using a logaritmhmic distribution for the radial coordinate. By convention, the first logical coordinate ( $\zeta$) is the radial one in each tree. The other two logical coordinates are then chosen as to form a right handed basis where the angular logical coordinates align with the axes of the physical coordinates in the embedding space. In particular, the coordinate transformation is constructed as follows. Let $L$ be half of the length of the side of the central Cartesian patch, $R_i$ be the radius of the inner spherical face and $R_o$ be the radius of the outer grid boundary. These variables can be chosen from the {\ttfamily GRACE} config file. We can then define the sphere and frustum functions as follows\+: \begin{eqnarray*} S &=& S_0 + \zeta~S_r, \\ F &=& F_0 + \zeta~F_r. \end{eqnarray*} Here the frustum rates $F_0,F_r$, and the sphere rates $S_0,S_r$ are defined as \begin{eqnarray*} F_0 &=& -r {\left(\mathit{s_i} - 1\right)}, \\ F_r &=& r {\left(\mathit{s_i} - 1\right)} - R {\left(\mathit{s_o} - 1\right)}, \\ S_0 &=& r \mathit{s_i}, \\ S_r &=& -r \mathit{s_i} + R \mathit{s_o}. \end{eqnarray*} Where $r$ is $L$ \mbox{[}\mbox{[}resp. $R_i$\mbox{]}\mbox{]} for the inner \mbox{[}\mbox{[}resp. outer\mbox{]}\mbox{]} and $R=R_i$ \mbox{[}\mbox{[}resp. $R_o$\mbox{]}\mbox{]}, whereas $\mathit{s_i},\mathit{s_o}$ are the sphericities of the inner and outer face which are $0,1$ for a flat or spherical face. We can now define the coordinate transformation which reads (in the case of the $+x$ wedge) \begin{eqnarray*} x &=& F + \frac{S}{\rho}~, \\ y &=& x~\left(2~\eta-1\right)~, \\ z &=& x~\left(2~\xi-1 \right)~. \end{eqnarray*} Where the function $\rho$ is given by \[ \rho=\sqrt{1+(2~\eta-1)^2+(2~\xi-1)^2}~. \] When the radial points are radially sampled, the frustum rates are zero and the sphere rates change to \begin{eqnarray*} S_0 &=& \frac{1}{2}\log(r~R)~, \\ S_r &=& \frac{1}{2}\log(R/r)~. \end{eqnarray*} And the radial coordinate is computed as \[ x = \frac{1}{\rho}~e^{S_0+S_r~\left(2~\zeta-1\right)}~. \]
